History

The Scuderia Ferrari, founded in 1929 by Enzo Ferrari (on the picture), has been the branch responsible for racing Ferrari cars since 1947. Nowadays, the Scuderia Ferrari focuses exclusively on competing in the Formula 1 World Championship, holding most of the records.

Iconic car

Designed to celebrate the 40th anniversary of the Ferrari brand, the F40 was the last car to be supervised by Enzo Ferrari. It also broke one of the most iconic records for a production car of its time by surpassing the 200 miles per hour mark.

Legendary pilot

Michael Schumacher is widely regarded as one of the most successful and iconic drivers in the history of Scuderia Ferrari. He joined the team in 1996 and went on to win five consecutive World Championships from 2000 to 2004. His partnership with Ferrari marked a period of dominance for the team.
